Software Guides

Increasing Productivity

From September we want to do things a little different in media studies in an effort to try and help you all stay of top of your workload as we start the new school year. Your mobile devices will be a huge part of this and as a result there are a few online services we would like to encourage everyone to start using.

Twitter

The media studies department has a twitter account which can be found @fccmediastudies, we would like every student to follow us. Twitter is a fantastic resource for a media student, not only will we be sharing a huge range of useful links to news stories, TV programmes and podcasts; but we will also be posting regular reminders of deadlines etc.

Even if you don't currently use Twitter, please consider creating an account, it is an ideal way of keeping up with the latest department information.

Google Drive

We have written about this before on the site but memory sticks are not indestructible and are prone to failure at some point. They also have a habit of being left at home! As a result we expect each student to have created a Google Drive account, which will be used to transport work between home and the college. You get 15GB of online storage, as well as an e-mail address - all for FREE!

You can sign up for a Google Account here.

Google Calendars

This isn't so much a service to sign up for, as a service to subscribe to. We have created Google Calendars for each year group and we will be posting deadlines etc to them. If you subscribe to the calendar on your phone, you will get reminders before assignments are due to be completed. Very helpful for staying on top of your workload!

You can find complete guides to subscribing to the calendars here.

Evernote

In particular this is a service we would like all of the GCSE students using but it will also be very useful for the A-Level students. Think of Evernote as your cloud based filing cabinet, as with Google Drive it is free to join up which gives you access to 60MB of storage each month. While this doesn't sound like a huge amount for our initial purposes it will do just fine, we would like everyone to get into the habit of snapping a photo of their notes at the end of every lesson. This will allow you to keep all of your notes safe should you ever misplace your exercise book.

By using the Evernote app on your phone, you can quickly take a photo and upload it to your Evernote account. The best thing about it is that it will automatically date stamp everything for you, which makes it easy to track down at a later date and allows you to keep all of your notes safe ready for revision.

We'll be posting more about Evernote over the next few weeks, but for now you can sign up for an Evernote account here.

That is it, four simple services which should help you remain productive and on top of your workload during the new school year.

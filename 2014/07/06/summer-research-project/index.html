Summer Research Project

This project is for the new year 12 students who will begin AS Media Studies in September 2014.

One of the options that you will have when you begin planning your practical projects during the 1st term, will be to create a viral marketing campaign for a TV programme of your own creation (you can find the full brief breakdown here). This particular option has been available to students for a number of years and yet is rarely chosen. One reason which is often given when asked why is often down to the fact that most students find it difficult to realise what a viral marketing campaign actually strives to achieve. This year I would like to try and change that, hopefully this summer project will help you to get a head start.

Task Description:

Carry out some research about Viral Marketing campaigns.

You should find one example of a viral marketing campaign.

Try to identify …
- what it was promoting
- how successful was the campaign
- who it’s target audience were
- why it appealed to that target audience

Below you can find two websites which may help you to get started with your research, however I am not just looking for a word for word copy of what is on these websites. A-Level Media Studies is about applying your own thoughts and opinions to the research. This project will form the basis of the first few lessons in September.

  • 15 of the best Viral Marketing Campaigns
  • Blair Witch and the rise of viral movie marketing

Just a friendly note: The Blair Witch was an important step forward in regard to viral marketing for film, however it is also extremely old now. Please aim to find a campaign which has been created in the past five years.

If you have any questions you can contact me via dale@felpham.org.uk, I will be checking my e-mail regularly during the summer break.

Have a great summer!

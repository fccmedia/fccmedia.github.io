Fall Out Boy complete the Young Blood Chronicles

The musical film project, which started in February of 2013, has finished with the release of the final two music videos on Saturday 24th. There are a couple of things to consider about this project:

  • This was a huge undertaking, releasing a music video for every song on an album is a rare event but there is a precedence of similar projects. During an interview with MTV Pete Wentz makes reference to Electroma by Daft Punk.

“In some ways, we were inspired by Daft Punk’s "Electroma" film, not literally inspired by it, but more the idea of the deceit within each of us that we have to uncover in the search for who we truly are,” Wentz wrote. “There will be a video for every song on the album … they’ll be able to be watched back-to-back and it will make sense if one has the stomach to sit through 11 FOB music videos in a row.”

  • Just as with a television series, the entire project is an example of segmented narrative. Each video can be viewed as a standalone component but also as a whole.
  • It is an excellent example of promotion, especially when you consider that the original album was released over a year ago. Yet they have managed to keep up the interest by offering updates via social networking and their official website.
  • There are some interesting uses of intertextuality - I think the briefcase is a nice nod to Pulp Fiction.
